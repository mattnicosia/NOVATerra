Every architect has a Revit model. Every estimator ignores it and counts from PDFs anyway.

I just changed that in NOVATerra.

Upload an IFC file. Click "Generate Takeoffs." Your estimate populates automatically.

Concrete goes to Division 03. Framing to Division 06. Steel to Division 05. Doors and windows to Division 08. Quantities come straight from the model. NetArea, Length, Volume. Metric converted to imperial on the way in.

The data was never missing. It was just locked in a format estimators couldn't use.

Now the 3D viewer links back to every element with an estimate item. You can see exactly what's covered and what isn't. No guessing.

You still have to price it. But the counting part. The part that takes two days on a big job. Done.

This is what happens when the person building the software has actually done the work it replaces.
