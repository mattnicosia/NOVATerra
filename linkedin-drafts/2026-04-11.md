Nine days since MVP shipped.

Steve's been using it on a real project. Real drawings, real takeoffs, real dollar amounts attached.

The first bug report came in within an hour. Then a second. Then a third. Not crashes. Just things that feel wrong when you actually use the tool for work instead of testing it yourself.

That gap between "it works" and "it works for someone else" is humbling every time.

I've fixed 13 issues since beta started. Most of them were invisible to me because I know the software. I know which buttons to press. I don't click in the same places a real user clicks.

That's the part nobody talks about. Shipping MVP isn't the finish line. It's the starting line for finding out what you actually built.

If you've built something and handed it to a real user for the first time, you know the feeling. That mix of pride and dread when the first message comes in.

What broke when you first put your product in someone else's hands?
