I've lost a bid because of software.

Not because my number was wrong. Because the takeoff didn't save right and I had to rebuild it the morning it was due.

Most estimating tools are built by people who've never priced a job. They don't know what it feels like to have a GC call you at 7am asking for a revised number. They don't know that "cloud sync" means nothing if your data gets overwritten mid-session.

The hardest part of building NOVATerra isn't the takeoff engine or the AI. It's the sync layer. Making sure that when two people are in the same estimate, nobody's work disappears.

Race conditions in software are invisible until they aren't. Then they're a lost bid.

I spent today fixing edge cases in the cloud sync that most tools would ship and forget. Because I've been on the other side of that bug.

If your software has ever eaten your work, you already know why I'm building this.
